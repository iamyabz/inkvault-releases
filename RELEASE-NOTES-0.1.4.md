## InkVault 0.1.4 (beta)

Turn your handwritten notes, PDFs, scans, lecture recordings, and spreadsheets
into one private, AI-searchable library — with exact page and timestamp citations.

### New in 0.1.4
- **Ask AI, three ways:** get a **Concise answer**, **Key Points**, or **Study Notes** — every one grounded in your evidence and cited. Switch modes on any answer; **Copy** to your notes.
- **Answers you can trust:** if nothing in your library strongly matches, InkVault won't guess — it shows the related sources and lets you **"Answer anyway,"** or tells you there's no match. It never sends your whole library to the AI.
- **Cleaner answers:** headings, bullets, and bold now render properly, with clickable citations to the exact page or audio timestamp.
- **Trial at a glance:** a "Trial — N days left" badge in the toolbar (it disappears once you enter a license).
- Fixes and polish across handwriting, scans, audio, research PDFs, and spreadsheets.

### Install
1. Open the `.dmg`, drag **InkVault** to **Applications**.
2. First launch: **right-click → Open → Open** (one-time macOS prompt).
3. Settings (⌘,) → AI Model → paste a free [Gemini key](https://aistudio.google.com/apikey).

Free for 14 days, full features, no code needed. Requires macOS 14+.
